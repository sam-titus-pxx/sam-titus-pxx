How To Grow Corn

by Fatherly P. Titus

25/02/2025

Planting

• Acquire the corn kernels to be planted.
• Soak the kernels overnight in a container of water for water to be absorbed through the hard kernel layer. (If using white corn kernels you can skip this step).
• Select a spot where the plant will not be shaded from sunlight. Corn needs atleast 7-8 hours of direct sunlight.
• The soil must be loamy and rich in humus (dead organic plant matter). Corn requires soil that is high in Nitrogen.
• Dig a small shallow square hole about 1foot wide and 2cm deep. Sow 4 corn kernels at each corner of the square hole and lightly cover with soil.
• TIP: Augment the quality of the soil by incorporating dead, finely shredded plant matter or compost to a depth of about 2 inches.

Growth

• Corn has a growing period of approximately 3 months from the time of germination.
• Requires constant watering.
• You can apply nitrogen fertilizer to the soil once every two weeks till maturity.

Tending

• Due to their long stalks, corn plants are very susceptible to being blown over by strong breeze or excess rain.
- Grow corn in blocks in order to prevent their blowing over by strong winds.
• Ensure to weed around the corn plants regularly to prevent overcrowding and overwhelming by weeds. • Once in awhile, mound the plant base with soil to increase plant stability.

Harvesting

• Harvest corn when the silks are dried off and brown indicating that adequate pollination has taken place and the cob is full.
• Leave corn cob in the sheath for storage until ready to be used.
• Store in a cool dry place without moisture or humidty.

Happy Farming
